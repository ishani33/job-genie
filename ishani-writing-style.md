## Core Style Rules

- **Length by stage:**
  - Connection request (cold, no connection yet): under 200 characters
  - First DM after connecting: 4-5 lines max
  - Follow-up (no reply): 2-3 lines max
  - After meeting/coffee chat: 5-6 lines max
  - Email (any stage): 8 lines max, always include subject line
- **One ask per message.** Never stack requests.
- **Specific hook first.** Always open with something real: where you met,
  a mutual connection, their specific role/team, a product detail, a
  company milestone, or a genuine coincidence. Never open with
  "I hope this finds you well" or "I wanted to reach out."
- **Warm but not gushing.** Genuine enthusiasm, not corporate enthusiasm.
  No "super excited", no "I am so passionate about."
- **Casual professional.** Contractions are fine. One exclamation mark
  per message max. No em dashes. No "just" as a softener.
- **No filler.** Cut "I wanted to", "I was hoping to", "I'd love to take
  the opportunity to." Just say the thing.
- **Low-friction ask.** "Would you be open to a quick 15 min chat?" not
  "Please let me know at your earliest convenience."
- **Sign off as "Ishani" only** for LinkedIn DMs. No title, no full name.
- **Use "Boothie" only when connecting with confirmed Booth alumni.**
  Never use it for non-Booth people.
- **Never mention wanting a referral directly** in a first outreach.
- **No em dashes anywhere.** Use a comma, period, or new sentence instead.

## Message Types and Patterns

### Connection Request (Cold, under 200 characters)
Pattern: [Hook] + [1-line credential] + [Soft ask to connect]
No ask for a chat yet. Just connect first.

### First DM After Connecting
Pattern: [Specific hook about them or their work] + [Brief credential
relevant to their world] + [Single low-friction ask]

### Follow-Up (No Reply, 2-3 lines)
Pattern: [Light bump] + [One line why still relevant] + [Same ask]
Never desperate. Never "just following up."

### Thank You + Referral Ask (Within 24hrs of meeting)
Pattern: [Specific thing from the conversation] + [Gratitude] +
[Natural referral ask, not transactional]
Never: "I wanted to formally request a referral."
Always: work it in naturally after showing you valued their time.

### Referral Email (Warm connection)
Pattern: [Personal opener] + [Why reaching out now] + [2-3 line
background] + [Specific ask: referral + intro if possible] +
[Offer to make it easy for them]

### Consumer Product Outreach (User POV)
Pattern: [Genuine specific user observation about their product/feature]
+ [Credentials] + [Soft ask]
Use when reaching out to someone at a consumer company you actually use.

- "I wanted to reach out"
- "I am super excited"
- "To jog your memory"
- "I wanted to formally request"
- "At your earliest convenience"
- "I was hoping to"
- "I truly appreciate your time and consideration"
- "It would be an honor"
- "Just" as a softener at the start of a sentence
- Em dashes (use comma or period instead)
- "This one stopped my scroll"
- "Boothie" for non-Booth people
